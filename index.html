CHAIRMAN BHUTTO HANGED
SANG-E-MEL
Special Edition
April 4, 1979
Chairman Zulfikar Ali Bhutto was executed at Central Jail in the early hours following the Supreme Court verdict. Authorities confirmed the sentence was carried out under heavy security arrangements.

Official sources stated that law and order remains under control.
The execution marks one of the most significant and controversial moments in the nation's political history. Security forces were deployed in major cities amid concerns of public reaction.

Political observers describe the event as a turning point that will shape the country's future direction.

Public gatherings were closely monitored as authorities urged citizens to remain calm and maintain order during this critical moment.

International reactions began to emerge as foreign governments closely watched developments within the country.
QUAID-E-AWAM LAID TO REST IN HIS ANCESTRAL GRAVEYARD NEAR NODERO
Burial Ceremony Held in Ancestral Village
The body of Quaid-e-Awam Zulfikar Ali Bhutto was transported under strict security arrangements to his ancestral village near Nodero where funeral prayers were offered quietly. Only close family members and selected officials were permitted to attend the burial under tight restrictions.
Thousands gathered outside the secured perimeter. Authorities monitored the situation closely and restricted public movement.
Funeral Conducted Under Extraordinary Security Measures
Eyewitnesses described the atmosphere as silent and tense. Security forces remained stationed throughout the area. Officials later confirmed that the burial proceedings concluded peacefully without incident.
